Grief

--name April 2, 2014

Grief is a natural response to loss. It's the emotional suffering you feel when someone or something you love is taken away. Often, the pain of loss can feel overwhelming. You may experience all kinds of difficult and unexpected emotions, from shock or anger to disbelief, guilt, and profound sadness.



All of us experience grief at some point in our lives. Every person reacts to grief differently, Some might think that the best way to cope is to spend some alone time and try to process their emotions while others may feel the need to surround themselves with their loved ones. Some may feel the need to visit a professional whereas some just need a friend to confide in. From experience, the best advice I can give you is to first accept the situation for what it is.You may experience an array of emotions and it might feel so overbearing that you feel like crying or sobbing to yourself and that is perfectly alright. You may have mood swings or sudden bursts of sadness and it’s important to recognize when you are feeling this way.



Spending time with close friends and family of the deceased is another great way of coping, sharing stories and cherishing special memories goes a long way. Another important aspect in the healing process is closure, this is necessary because, without closure, we tend to get stuck in a cycle of grief. Attending and taking part in the final rites of the deceased can help you accept your loss and move on. We must also understand that acceptance does not mean you have forgotten the person and what they meant to you, it just means that you have come to terms with your loss and are preparing to move forward in life.



Respect everyone’s experiences and grief and always try to help others who are in a similar situation as you were. Be cognizant of important dates and continue to honour and cherish the life of your loved ones.
